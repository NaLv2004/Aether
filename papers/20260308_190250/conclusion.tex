\section{Conclusion}

This paper has successfully developed and validated the IB-CellFree-GNN framework, a novel task-oriented communication paradigm designed to overcome the severe backhaul capacity constraints inherent in 6G cell-free massive MIMO networks. By integrating a Gumbel-Softmax-based successive refinement policy network at the distributed access points with a bit-aware heterogeneous graph neural network at the central processing unit, the proposed architecture dynamically optimizes local quantization bit-widths and robustly mitigates multi-user interference under asymmetric noise conditions. Extensive evaluations highlight the exceptional efficiency of the framework, which achieves near-optimal 32-bit full-precision detection performance while consuming an average of only 2.5 bits per payload. Additionally, the system demonstrates extreme resilience to link failure rates as high as fifty percent and proves highly scalable by maintaining a constant, order-one artificial intelligence computational complexity regardless of massive antenna deployments. Future research will focus on extending this framework to fully decentralized graph neural network architectures to further alleviate central processing bottlenecks, alongside conducting hardware-in-the-loop prototype validations to assess its practical viability in real-world environments.
