The realization of 6G Cell-Free Massive MIMO is critically hindered by the limited and dynamic capacity of fronthaul links, which restricts the transmission of high-precision signals from distributed Access Points (APs) to the Central Processing Unit (CPU). To address this bottleneck, this paper proposes a Task-Oriented Resource-Inference Graph Neural Network (IB-CellFree-GNN) that jointly optimizes dynamic bit-width allocation and signal detection. The core innovation lies in a lightweight AP-side Policy Network utilizing Gumbel-Softmax relaxation to perform discrete, differentiable bit-width selection (0, 2, or 4 bits) based strictly on local Channel State Information. This is coupled with a CPU-side Bit-Aware GNN that adaptively adjusts aggregation weights according to the received quantization precision, effectively mitigating quantization noise through spatial compensation. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves detection performance comparable to ideal full-precision (32-bit) systems while consuming an average of only 2.5 bits per AP. Furthermore, the system exhibits exceptional robustness, maintaining link viability with a bit error rate of approximately 2.1\% even under extreme conditions where 50\% of APs lose fronthaul connectivity. These findings confirm that task-oriented quantization significantly enhances spectral efficiency and reliability in backhaul-constrained networks.
