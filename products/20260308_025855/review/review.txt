# Review Report for IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology

**Paper Title:** Joint Optimization of Signal Quantization and Detection in Cell-Free MIMO: A Quantization Aware GNN Approach
**Authors:** Aether
**Recommendation:** **Reject** (with encouragement to resubmit as a full Regular Paper after major expansions)

## 1. Brief Summary
This paper investigates the uplink detection problem in Cell-Free Massive MIMO (CF-MIMO) systems constrained by limited fronthaul capacity. The authors propose a Joint Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) framework. At the AP side, a triple-head Policy Network uses Gumbel-Softmax relaxation to adaptively assign quantization bit-widths to the received signal, channel estimates, and local LMMSE estimates. At the CPU side, a Mean-Field Graph Neural Network (GNN) combined with a Bit-Aware Transformer aggregates the quantized signals for final multi-user detection. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed method approaches full-precision performance while significantly reducing fronthaul load.

## 2. Overall Assessment
The paper tackles a highly relevant and critical bottleneck in practical CF-MIMO deployments: fronthaul capacity limitation. The idea of jointly optimizing task-oriented quantization at the edge (APs) and bit-aware detection at the central node (CPU) using deep learning is novel, well-motivated, and aligns perfectly with the scope of IEEE TVT.

However, as a manuscript generated by AI, it exhibits the classic symptoms of LLM-authored academic text: it reads more like an extended abstract or a high-level grant proposal than a rigorous IEEE Transactions journal paper. The mathematical formulations are superficial, the neural network architectures are treated as black boxes without concrete definitions, the baselines are inadequately chosen, and the crucial details required for reproducibility (e.g., training dataset, hyperparameters) are entirely missing. 

To meet the high standards of IEEE TVT, the manuscript requires substantial human intervention to provide mathematical depth, rigorous baseline comparisons, and comprehensive system modeling.

## 3. Major Comments (Mandatory Corrections)

**3.1. Lack of Architectural Rigor and Mathematical Depth**
The descriptions of the proposed Neural Networks are severely lacking in mathematical detail. 
* In Section III-A, the "triple-head Policy Network $\pi_\phi$" is introduced, but its architecture (number of layers, activation functions, input dimension) is not defined. What exactly is the feature vector $a_l$? How are the SNR and condition numbers calculated locally?
* In Equation (7), the GNN is defined simply as `AGG(MLP(...))`. For an IEEE TVT paper, the exact message-passing functions, update functions, and the choice of the aggregation operator (e.g., SUM, MEAN, MAX) must be explicitly mathematically formulated. 
* Equation (8) introduces a Bit-Aware MHA, but it is unclear how $Qz_k$ and $Kz_l$ are generated. Are these standard queries and keys from a Transformer? If so, what are their dimensions?

**3.2. Unfair and Incomplete Baseline Comparisons**
* In Section I-A (Related Work), the authors cite SOTA deep learning MIMO detectors such as DetNet [7] and OAMP-Net2 [8]. However, in Section IV (Numerical Results), the proposed highly non-linear GNN-Transformer is only compared against linear baselines (C-MMSE, MMSE-PIC). It is inherently unfair to compare a deep learning detector's BER against a linear receiver. The authors must include unquantized and quantized versions of SOTA deep learning baselines (e.g., GNN-based detectors [9] or OAMP-Net2) to prove the superiority of the proposed architecture.
* What exactly is `C-MMSE-MxQ`? The baseline is introduced in one sentence and is not mathematically defined. How is the "non-uniform bit allocation based on large-scale fading" formulated?

**3.3. Zero Reproducibility (Missing Training Details)**
The paper claims "end-to-end training," but provides zero information on the training process. For a reproducible DL-based communication paper, the authors must specify:
* How was the dataset generated? How many samples for training, validation, and testing?
* What optimizer was used (e.g., Adam)? What was the learning rate?
* How were the hyper-parameters in Equation (5) ($\lambda$) and Equation (6) ($\tau$, the temperature) chosen or annealed during training? The tuning of the penalty factor $\lambda$ is critical for the Pareto front in Fig. 4, yet the methodology is omitted.

**3.4. System Model Inconsistencies**
* In Section II-B, the authors state that each AP computes a local LMMSE estimate $\hat{s}_l = W_l y_l$. However, computing the true LMMSE matrix $W_l$ requires knowledge of the channel statistics of *all* users and their interference covariance. In a decentralized AP setup without CPU feedback, how does an AP accurately compute $W_l$? 
* The channel model assumes i.i.d. Rayleigh fast fading on top of UMi path loss. Practical CF-MIMO systems (especially with $N=4$ antennas per AP) exhibit strong spatial correlation. The channel model should be updated to include spatial correlation matrices (e.g., the Kronecker model), as this heavily impacts the compressibility of the channel estimates $H$.

**3.5. Misleading Complexity Analysis**
Figure 6 compares the FLOPs of "LMMSE (Matrix Inv.)" against the "Policy Net (Proposed)". This is a fundamentally flawed comparison. The Policy Network only performs bit-allocation at the AP; the heavy lifting of multi-user detection is done by the GNN and Transformer at the CPU. To claim computational efficiency, the authors *must* calculate and plot the total end-to-end inference FLOPs (Policy Net at AP + GNN/Transformer at CPU) and compare it against the centralized MMSE-PIC baseline. 

## 4. Minor Comments

1. **Notation Error in Eq. (1):** The term $\sum_{k=1}^K \sqrt{p} h_{lk} s_k + n_l = H_l s + n_l$ requires a formal definition of $H_l$. It should be explicitly stated that $H_l = [h_{l1}, \dots, h_{lK}] \in \mathbb{C}^{N \times K}$, and the dimensions of $s$ should be clearly noted.
2. **Equation (2) formulation:** The syntax inside the `round()` function uses a discrete multiplication $\cdot s$ which might cause gradient issues depending on how the Straight-Through Estimator (STE) is implemented. Please provide a brief 1-2 sentence explanation of the specific STE implementation used (e.g., `s_quant = s_continuous + stop_gradient(s_discrete - s_continuous)`).
3. **Figure 3 Labeling:** The legend says "Dist-Full (K=12)". Please clarify if this is an unquantized baseline.
4. **Length and Format:** 4 pages is too short for a standard TVT regular submission. The authors should expand this into a 10-12 page double-column Regular Paper, utilizing the extra space to address all the architectural, training, and mathematical derivations requested above.

## Conclusion for the Authors
The proposed concept of integrating task-oriented communication principles with GNN-based detection for CF-MIMO is excellent and highly relevant. However, the current manuscript lacks the mathematical rigor, explicit network definitions, fair baseline comparisons, and training details expected by IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology. We strongly encourage the authors to thoroughly expand the mathematical models, conduct comparisons against SOTA Deep Learning baselines, provide complete training details, and resubmit as a full-length regular paper.
