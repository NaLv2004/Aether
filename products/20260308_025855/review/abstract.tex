Cell-Free Massive Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) systems achieve superior spectral efficiency through distributed Access Point (AP) cooperation, but their practical deployment is severely constrained by limited fronthaul capacity. This paper proposes a Joint Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) framework that co-optimizes adaptive signal quantization at the APs and centralized detection at the Central Processing Unit (CPU). At the AP side, a triple-head Policy Network employs Gumbel-Softmax relaxation to dynamically select optimal quantization bit-widths from the set $\{0, 4, 8, 12, 16\}$ for received signals, channel estimates, and local demodulation results based on instantaneous channel conditions. At the CPU side, a Mean-Field Graph Neural Network (GNN) detector, integrated with a multi-head Transformer, robustly combines heterogeneous-precision signals for multi-user interference cancellation. The entire framework is trained end-to-end with a task-oriented loss that minimizes detection error subject to a fronthaul bit-budget constraint. Numerical evaluations, averaged over multiple large-scale fading realizations under the 3GPP TR 36.814 (UMi) model, demonstrate that the proposed QAT-GNN achieves Bit Error Rate (BER) performance comparable to full-precision baselines (e.g., MMSE-PIC) while reducing the fronthaul load by over $29\times$. The framework exhibits graceful degradation under link failures (maintaining connectivity at $50\%$ AP dropout) and remarkable robustness to imperfect Channel State Information (CSI) and pilot contamination, significantly outperforming classical linear detectors.
