Massive connectivity in Cell-Free MIMO systems is significantly constrained by limited fronthaul capacity, where traditional fixed-quantization schemes often fail to adapt to dynamic channel conditions. To address this, we propose a Task-Oriented Resource-Inference Graph Neural Network (GNN) framework that jointly optimizes bit-width allocation and signal detection. Unlike conventional methods that separate quantization from detection, our approach deploys a lightweight, local Policy Network at each Access Point (AP) to infer the optimal quantization bit-width ($0$, $2$, or $4$ bits) based on instantaneous local Channel State Information (CSI), coupled with a central Bit-Aware GNN for robust signal detection. The framework leverages Gumbel-Softmax relaxation to enable differentiable discrete bit selection and employs Learned Step Size Quantization (LSQ) to minimize quantization error during training. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed system achieves detection performance comparable to full-precision $32$-bit baselines while consuming only approximately $2.5$ bits on average. Furthermore, the framework exhibits enhanced robustness, maintaining a Bit Error Rate (BER) below $2.2\%$ even when $50\%$ of APs are disconnected, a scenario where traditional fixed-quantization schemes experience significant performance degradation.
